Facts about the Largest Stars in the Universe

1. UY Scuti is the Largest Known Star

UY Scuti is a red supergiant star located in the constellation Scutum. It holds the title of being the largest known star by radius.

2. VY Canis Majoris: One of the Most Massive Stars

VY Canis Majoris is a red hypergiant star in the constellation Canis Major. It is one of the most massive stars known, with a radius that could extend past the orbit of Jupiter.

3. Antares: A Red Supergiant Star

An international team of astronomers from Chile and Germany has managed to capture an image of unprecedented detail of another star — that isn’t the Sun — the red supergiant star Antares. It is one of the brightest stars in the night sky and has a radius larger than the orbit of Mars.

4. Betelgeuse: A Notable Variable Star

Betelgeuse is one of the brightest stars in the night sky and also one of the largest stars known to astronomers. Forming the left shoulder of the constellation Orion, Betelgeuse is a so-called red giant, a star in the final stages of its life, which sometimes prompts speculations that it might soon explode in a supernova.

5. Mu Cephei: A Prototype of the "Mu Cephei Variables"

Mu Cephei, also known as the Garnet Star, is a red supergiant star in the constellation Cepheus. It is a prototype of the Mu Cephei variables, a class of pulsating variable stars.
